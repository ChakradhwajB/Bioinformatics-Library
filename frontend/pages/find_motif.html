Educational Guide: Motif Finding & Spotlight Vectors

In bioinformatics, locating specific sequence motifs within larger genomic sequences is a crucial task for understanding transcriptional regulation, gene expression, and DNA-protein binding interactions.

1. Sequence Pattern Matching

A sequence motif is a short, recurring pattern of nucleotides or amino acids that has biological significance. The motif finder performs a linear search over a genome sequence $S$ of length $n$ to identify all occurrences of a target motif sequence $M$ of length $m$. Matches are highlighted in red.

2. Density Track & Relative Mapping

The density sidebar provides a spatial heatmap of motif density across the sequence length. Selecting any marker within this sidebar track scrolls the main viewer directly to the corresponding match position.

3. Algorithmic Complexity Analysis

The naive search operates in $\mathcal{O}(n \cdot m)$ worst-case time complexity, checking character matches at each sliding window offset. The space complexity is $\mathcal{O}(n)$ to hold the visual highlights and coordinate maps.
